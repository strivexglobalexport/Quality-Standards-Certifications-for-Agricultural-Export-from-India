## ðŸ›ï¸ Regulatory & Institutional Framework in India

Understanding Indiaâ€™s export regulatory ecosystem is crucial before you start.

### Import-Export Code (IEC)
- **Issued by:** DGFT  
- **Purpose:** Mandatory for all exporters; without it, exports are not legally permitted.

### APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority)
- Central body overseeing export of scheduled agri & processed food items.  
- Promotes infrastructure, market development, and ensures standards.  
- Administers **TraceNet**, an electronic traceability platform for organic exports.  
- Accredits organic certification bodies under **NPOP** (National Programme for Organic Production).

### RCMC (Registration-cum-Membership Certificate)
- Received after APEDA registration.  
- Required for export incentives and trade benefits.  
- As of July 2023, issued through the **DGFT portal**.

### Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
APEDA publishes **SOPs** for exports (e.g., grapes, peanuts) including residue testing, documentation, and certification steps.

### Government Support
Under **Agriculture Export Promotion Scheme (AEPS)**, APEDA offers financial assistance for:
- Quality development  
- Market development  
- Infrastructure support

## âœ… Key Quality Standards & Certifications

Below are the core certifications that agriculture exporters in India must or should consider.

### AGMARK (Agricultural Marketing Standards)
- **Issued by:** Directorate of Marketing & Inspection, Ministry of Agriculture  
- Certifies quality (grading, purity, moisture, etc.) for 224+ commodities.  
- **Benefit:** Enhances acceptability and affirms product integrity.

### FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India)
- Applies mainly to **processed or edible food items**.  
- Ensures hygiene, labeling, and traceability compliance.

### Phytosanitary Certificate
- Mandatory for **plant products** (fresh fruits, vegetables, seeds, live plants).  
- **Issued by:** National Plant Protection Organization (NPPO).  
- Certifies products are pest- and disease-free.

### Organic Certification / NPOP / India Organic
- For organic produce, certification under **NPOP** ensures compliance.  
- Products bear the **India Organic** mark.  
- Recognized by **EU and US** for equivalence.

### FPO (Fruit Products Order) Mark
- Mandatory for processed fruits & vegetables (juices, jams, pickles, etc.).  
- Focuses on purity, hygiene, and labeling.

### ISO & Voluntary / Niche Standards
- **ISO 22000**: Food Safety Management  
- **ISO 9001**: Quality Management  
- **GlobalG.A.P, Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance**: For sustainability and premium markets.

**Q: What certifications are mandatory for agricultural export from India?**  
A: Typically, APEDA registration (with RCMC), phytosanitary certificate (for plants), AGMARK or relevant marks, FSSAI (for food items), and if organic, NPOP certification.

**Q: How to get an AGMARK certification for export?**  
A: Apply to the Directorate of Marketing & Inspection, submit samples, follow grading norms, and comply with documentation and audits.

**Q: Is FSSAI license required for exporting fruits or grains?**  
A: FSSAI applies mainly to processed or edible items. Fresh produce relies more on phytosanitary certification.

**Q: What is a phytosanitary certificate and how to obtain it?**  
A: It verifies plant products are pest-free. Request inspection via NPPO; certification is issued if cleared.

**Q: How long does organic certification (NPOP) take in India?**  
A: Generally takes a year or more, depending on supply chain and readiness.
